Art

As life grows, we realise that personal achievement are not always palpable. Sometimes overcoming individual obstacles is what changes your life for good. As a kid I loved painting, it was a part of my everyday schedule. But life happened, painting had to take a backseat. But during this lockdown, everything was back to square one. It was no more about the material things, but, survival. So one day while cleaning my cupboard,I stumbled across my old art book and it sparked something. I decided to paint again.

Initially it was tough, I was a little rusty but eventually I found my footing. It took me hours to come out of the block but it eventually happened. After that first day, I decided to go on a 100 day painting spree wherein I would draw something new every day for the next 100 days. With this, I had achieved the hardest thing, I started. Even if it was something as small as stick figures, I did draw for a hundred days and when pages seemed too restricting I painted on something as small as a credit card. When there was a scarcity of inspiration, I would often draw people walking across streets. Even though a normal viewpoint was good, life seemed different with an artist’s perspective. There was a story in everything for me to tell and if, I wasn’t, I was able to create a narration. By the end, I had a beautiful art collection. This whole experience taught me something amazing, if I set my mind on something and am consistent, I will achieve it. One is never too busy to make time for things that matter and determination is the key. This entire experience paved a way for a better, free and holistic world for me.
